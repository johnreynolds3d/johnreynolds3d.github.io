What Now?
Changing Goals and Moving Toward the Future

17 NOVEMBER 2017

In 2016, as my time at the University of Western Australia drew to a close, I found my interest shifting from Artificial Intelligence toward Virtual Reality. A fascination with the concept of the Technological Singularity had been a major factor in my motivation to study Computer Science, but after building some simple VR apps in Unity, I was hooked!

After reading (OK, listening to on Audible) the obligatory VR primers — Neal Stephenson's Snow Crash, and Ernest Cline's Ready Player One — and while looking for inspiration in the works of prominent artists and designers, I stumbled across Ray Caesar.

I fell immediately in love with his aesthetic, but I was staggered to learn that his images were not, as I had assumed, two-dimensional digital paintings, but renderings of 3D models set in painstakingly textured environments; each character rigged with a poseable 'skeleton' and lit as if on the set of a motion picture.

In an interview, the artist fondly recalled returning to one of these 'scenes' after several years and discovering a letter he had penned to his wife, left in a digital chest of drawers and long since forgotten.

I eagerly immersed myself in learning the fundamentals of this seemingly boundless creative medium with Autodesk's Maya (Mr. Caesar's tool of choice), then The Foundry's Modo, finally settling on the free and open-source Blender.

Then I found out about WebVR. I hadn't been all that taken with Web Development during the brief overview that my degree's schedule afforded, but the idea that these virtual worlds could be delivered on a ubiquitous platform like the web sealed the deal for me.

The goal of this website is to act as my portfolio as I learn how to build something resembling at least one small corner of the Metaverse.
